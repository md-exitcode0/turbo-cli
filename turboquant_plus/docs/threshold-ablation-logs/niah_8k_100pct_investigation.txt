NIAH 8K/100% Failure Investigation
====================================

Date: 2026-03-28

Finding: Both q8_0 and turbo4 fail NIAH at 8K context, 100% depth (needle
at end of context). f16 passes. This is quantization-related, not model-level.

100% depth sweep:
| Context | f16 | q8_0 | turbo4 |
|---------|-----|------|--------|
| 2K | — | PASS | PASS |
| 4K | — | PASS | PASS |
| 8K | PASS | FAIL | FAIL |
| 16K | — | FAIL | PASS |

turbo4 recovers at 16K while q8_0 doesn't — turbo4 has less quantization
noise (lower KLD: 0.0096 vs q8_0's 0.0015... wait q8_0 has LOWER KLD).

Root cause: 100% depth = needle inserted after last paragraph = last few
hundred tokens of context. These are the most recently written KV entries,
most vulnerable to quantization noise. At shorter context (2K/4K) the noise
is tolerable. At 8K+ it crosses the retrieval threshold.

This is documented behavior for quantized KV caches. Not fixable without
changing the quantization scheme. Both q8_0 and turbo4 share this failure.

f16 passes because it has zero quantization noise.

Action: Document in NIAH results. Not a turbo4-specific issue.
