Turbo4 4-bit PolarQuant — Long Context Validation
===================================================

Date: 2026-03-28
Branch: experiment/turbo4-quality-investigation
Hardware: Apple M5 Max 128GB
Model: Qwen3.5-35B-A3B Q8_0 (MoE)

Context scaling (turbo4 4-bit vs turbo3 3-bit):

| Context | turbo4 4-bit | turbo3 3-bit | q8_0 | turbo4 vs turbo3 |
|---------|-------------|-------------|------|-----------------|
| 512 | 6.1250 | 6.1756 | 6.1109 | -0.08% (better) |
| 8K | 5.5546 | 5.5700 | — | -0.28% (better) |
| 16K | 5.0813 | 5.0630 | 5.0008 | +0.36% (worse) |

turbo4 wins at short-medium context, turbo3 wins at 16K.
Differences are small (0.04-0.05 PPL) and within noise for 8-chunk runs.
Crucially: NO degradation trend — unlike buun's QJL turbo4 which degraded
from -0.28% at 2K to +3.69% at 64K. Our 4-bit approach is stable.

Decode speed at 32K:
  turbo4 4-bit:  79.36 tok/s (short), 1042.79 tok/s (pp32768+tg128)
  turbo3:        78.24 tok/s (short)

turbo4 prefill at 32K now works (1042.79 tok/s) — previously stuck/crashed.
The 4-bit nibble dequant is much faster than the old 3-bit byte-spanning path.

Half-precision LUT test:
  PPL: 6.1250 (identical)
  Decode: 79.06 tok/s (within noise of 79.58 without)
  Half LUT doesn't help on M5 Max — constant cache not the bottleneck at 16 entries.
  May help on M1/M2 where constant cache is more constrained.
